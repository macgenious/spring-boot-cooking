1. Honey never spoils; archaeologists have discovered 3,000-year-old jars of honey in Egyptian tombs that remain perfectly edible.

2. Tomatoes are botanically classified as fruits but are legally and culinarily treated as vegetables due to their savory flavor profile.

3. Dark chocolate contains high levels of flavonoids, antioxidants that support cardiovascular health and improve blood flow.

4. Bananas emit trace amounts of natural radiation from potassium-40, but you would need to eat millions at once for it to be harmful.

5. Sea salt retains trace minerals like magnesium and calcium, which enhance its briny flavor and make it less processed than refined table salt.

6. Capsaicin in spicy foods triggers heat receptors, prompting the brain to release endorphins that create a natural, pleasurable "high."

7. Avocados are technically single-seeded berries packed with monounsaturated fats that help lower bad cholesterol and support brain function.

8. Fermented foods like kimchi, kefir, and yogurt contain live probiotics that strengthen the gut microbiome and boost immune response.

9. Umami, the fifth basic taste described as deeply savory or delicious, comes from naturally occurring glutamates in aged cheeses, mushrooms, and soy sauce.

10. Carrots were originally cultivated as purple or yellow roots in Central Asia; the familiar orange variety was selectively bred by Dutch farmers in the 17th century.
