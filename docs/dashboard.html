Did you know?

Fact 01

Honey never spoils — archaeologists found 3,000-year-old honey in Egyptian tombs that was still perfectly edible.

Fact 02

Tomatoes are botanically fruits but legally treated as vegetables due to their savory flavor profile.

Fact 03

Dark chocolate contains flavonoids — antioxidants that support cardiovascular health and improve blood flow.

Fact 04

Bananas emit trace radiation from potassium-40, but you'd need to eat millions at once for it to be harmful.

Fact 05

Sea salt retains trace minerals like magnesium and calcium, giving it a richer, brinier flavor than table salt.

Fact 06

Capsaicin in spicy foods triggers heat receptors, prompting the brain to release endorphins — a natural high.

Fact 07

Avocados are technically single-seeded berries packed with monounsaturated fats that lower bad cholesterol.

Fact 08

Fermented foods like kimchi, kefir, and yogurt contain live probiotics that strengthen the gut microbiome.

Fact 09

Umami — the fifth basic taste — comes from glutamates found naturally in aged cheeses, mushrooms, and soy sauce.

Fact 10

Carrots were originally purple or yellow — the orange variety was selectively bred by Dutch farmers in the 17th century.
